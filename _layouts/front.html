Learning Music Style Through Cross-Modal Bootstrapping

ISMIR 2025 Submission #320

Abstract: What is music style? Though often described using text labels such as "swing," "classical," or "emotional," the real style remains implicit and hidden in concrete music examples. In this paper, we introduce a cross-modal framework that learns implicit music styles from raw audio and applies the styles to symbolic music generation. Inspired by BLIP-2, our model uses a Querying Transformer (Q-Former) to extract style representations from an audio LLM and further apply them to condition a symbolic LLM for generating piano arrangements. We adopt a two-stage training strategy: contrastive learning to align style representations with symbolic targets, followed by generative modeling to perform music arrangement. Our model generates piano performances jointly conditioned on a lead sheet (content) and a reference audio example (style), enabling controllable and stylistically faithful arrangement. We demonstrate its effectiveness on piano cover generation and style transfer tasks, achieving improved style-aware audio-to-symbolic coherence and overall music quality.

Piano Cover Generation

In this section, we showcase our model's capability in the piano cover generation task. Given an audio clip of a song, our model generates a symbolic piano arrangement that captures the feel from the original audio. The demo pieces are drawn from the Ballroom, RWC-Pop, and POP909 datasets, covering a wide range of genres and instrumentation. We compare our model against three baselines: 1) PiCoGen2, 2) Audio2MIDI, and 3) a variant of our model w/o pre-training. To ensure a fair comparison, the lead sheet input to our model is automatically transcribed by Sheetsage, making audio the sole input modality for all methods. For each model, we cherry-pick the best result from three generated samples.
